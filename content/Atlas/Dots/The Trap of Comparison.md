Recently I’ve been feeling more and more anxious and restless. Every day there seem to be so many things happening and so many opportunities appearing. Instead of feeling excited about them, I often feel *overwhelmed* and *tortured* by the choices.

During my latest job search, I received four offers, and all of them were quite good. That should have been a fortunate situation, but instead it made the decision extremely difficult. Every option had its pros and cons, and I kept wishing that I could somehow **combine all the advantages into one perfect choice**. Because of that mindset, I kept asking many people for advice and thinking endlessly about the trade-offs.

Eventually I made a decision and joined my current company. But after joining, I started thinking about all the “what ifs.” I kept imagining what it would have been like if I had chosen one of the other options.

At the same time, I began comparing the benefits at my current company with the ones I had at my previous company. What makes this worse is that when I was in my previous company, I never realized those things were actually benefits. I simply *took them for granted*: free dinner with good quality food, a fridge always filled with drinks, plenty of snacks, and various monetary perks. The workload was lighter and the schedule was more relaxed.

Now in the new company, many of those things are gone. The drinks are limited, there are almost no snacks, there is no free meal, and even simple things like a standing desk are not available. These changes feel surprisingly painful to me. I also feel dissatisfied with the monetary side compared to what I had expected.

Objectively speaking, this company still gives me many valuable things: strong growth opportunities, helpful guidance, and colleagues who are capable and supportive. The environment allows me to learn and improve steadily. But instead of focusing on these positives, I often find myself obsessing over what I have lost.

The problem becomes worse whenever I hear about other people’s companies. Sometimes I manage to convince myself that my situation is actually fine — that I can grow here, learn a lot, and progress at my own pace. But the moment I hear others talking about their benefits, such as remote work, travel opportunities, higher pay, or other perks, my perspective *collapses* again. Suddenly my job feels terrible, and even my life starts to feel meaningless in comparison.

I’ve realized that this pattern extends beyond work. In many areas of life, **my sense of happiness and contentment seems to be defined entirely by comparison with others**. I don’t seem to have my own internal system for evaluating what is “enough” or what should make me feel satisfied.

Because of that, my sense of contentment is extremely unstable. I might feel okay for a moment, but the next moment, after seeing someone else’s situation, everything falls apart again. Then the feelings of regret, comparison, and self-doubt come back very strongly.

I don’t want to continue living this way. I know something about this mindset needs to change.
